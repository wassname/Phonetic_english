About PIE (International)

Phonetically Intuitive English, International Edition is a Chrome extension that can automatically add diacritics to English words to make their pronunciation crystal clear, based on a one-page, easy-to-learn scheme (PDF version, image version). This International Edition is intended for people who learn English as a second language (ESL) to learn correct pronunciation (and pronunciation is also key to spelling); therefore the scheme mimics the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA) which is familiar to most ESL learners around the world. We also provide an American Edition for American children learning to read, which uses a slightly different scheme.

People who learn English as a second language may want to know a word's meaning in addition to its pronunciation. To address this need, we also provide a free Chrome extension called Word Translator that can translate the English word under the user's mouse to his native language. This Chrome extension can recognize English words already transformed to the PIE form, and therefore is a highly relevant companion for PIE (International).

Text Transformation Options

Depending on the learner's English level, PIE provides five modes:

The -i digraphs represent /ʃ/, /tʃ/, /ʒ/ or /dʒ/ before vowels:
 
cias in "efficient"
sias in "controversial"
tias in "patience"
dias in "soldier"
gias in "religious"
 
When necessary, PIE shows these consonants on the first letter (c, s, t, d or g), and the second letter (i) becomes silent but its silence mark is omitted for simplicity.

Native speakers usually speak function words in a "weak form". PIE's Full mode shows this form by default (except for "have" and "do", which are not always function words). Be aware that sometimes you must use the strong form.

Some words have multiple pronunciations each indicating a different meaning (e.g. "present"). PIE can optionally show a small plus sign (e.g. "present⁺") to highlight such cases.

Some words contain a pair of characters that may be misinterpreted as a digraph (e.g. "lighthouse"). PIE can optionally show a small dot (e.g. "light·house") to clarify such cases.

Getting a Word's Meaning

For people who learn English as a second language, PIE can show words' pronunciation but not meaning. To address this need, we also provide a free Chrome extension called Word Translator that can translate the English word under the user's mouse to his native language. This Chrome extension can recognize English words already transformed to the PIE form, and therefore is a highly relevant companion for PIE (International).

Font for PIE Text

By default, PIE uses Cambria for Windows Vista and higher, and Cherry PIE for Mac OS X, Linux and lower versions of Windows. If you want to specify your own font, we strongly recommend that you read our list of recommended fonts that you can install and use with PIE, because not all fonts are advanced enough to render PIE text correctly. As an extreme case, using Cambria with PIE in Mac OS X can even crash your tabs. So if anything goes wrong, switch back to the default option.

Page Zooming

To display PIE text more clearly, you can use two zoom features in Chrome:

  • To zoom the current site: Hold down the  Ctrl  key (or  ⌘  for Mac) and scroll your mouse wheel forward/backward to zoom in/out the current site. If you don't have a mouse, press  Ctrl  and  +  to zoom in,  Ctrl  and  -  to zoom out.
  • To zoom all sites: Go to the Chrome menu (  ) >  Settings  >  Show advanced settings...  >  Web content  >  Page zoom  and change this setting from  100%  to  125%  (or other zoom levels you like).
