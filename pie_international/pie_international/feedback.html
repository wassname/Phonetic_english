Institutions' Feedback

  • The United States Department of Education's Office of Elementary and Secondary Education (OESE) has a kind message regarding PIE:

    From: OESE <OESE@ed.gov>
    To: Ziyuan Yao <yaoziyuan@gmail.com>
    Sender: "Brooks, Janice" <Janice.Brooks@ed.gov>
    Date: Tue, 19 Jun 2012 10:18:50 -0500
    Subject: RE: A Chrome browser extension that may make literacy a solved problem

    Thank you for sharing information on your innovative Phonetically Intuitive English (PIE) program, design to improve English language proficiency for stduents and adult learners. Your letter was referred to the Office of Elementary and Secondary Education for review and I am pleased to respond.

    I have read your letter with interest and appreciate the thought and effort you have put into the PIE program. The U.S. Department of Education (the Department) supports the use of innovative programs to help all students achieve. However, the Department does not have the authority to determine the program of instruction, curriculum, disciplinary policy, or graduation requirements used in states and local schools. State departments of education and local educational agencies (LEAs) are responsible for making these decisions. Therefore, I recommend that you contact your local school district and make them aware of your program and its potential benefits you wish to make via recommendations and/or program improvements.

    Thank you for your commitment to public education. We wish you the best of successful with your future endeavors.

    Sincerely,

    Janice D. Brooks
    OESE Correspondence Unit

    PIE went there and got very positive feedback:

    “There was a very positive response from the expo attendees with whom I spoke. I had laid out screenshots of the program in action and the consistent reaction I encountered was one of being impressed. People easily saw the application's usefulness and appreciated its ease of use. [...] The educators I spoke with were from all across the country. Some were teachers, others administrators, others retired. Those who were involved with ESL were quick to appreciate PIE's offering. I had several people tell me that they were very familiar with diacritics, but hadn't seen any that were as accessible as PIE's, and no one claimed to have seen a program of similar capability.”

    Adrian Lopez
    Representative of PIE to NEA Expo 2013

  • The 7th largest school district in the United States—Broward County Public Schools—formally approved that PIE might be provided to schools in this district:

    October 2, 2013

    Dear Mr. Yao:

    The Superintendent’s Screening Committee has reviewed the request to provide Phonetically Intuitive English (PIE) to Broward County Schools. This request has been determined to be appropriate and in accordance with the curriculum in place in our schools and may be provided at the discretion of each individual principal.

    This approval is valid for 1 year (see expiration date below). To obtain a renewal, please submit a screening renewal application at least 4 weeks prior to this expiration date. All approved programs will be required to provide an assessment of program effectiveness prior to obtaining a renewal. The assessment tool is attached.

    Please note: Although the program is approved for use in Broward County Public Schools, this approval in no way reflects an endorsement of your program by the School Board of Broward County, Florida. Also, please be aware that contacting schools for participation is the responsibility of the applicant unless other arrangements have been made with one of our departments. Mailing labels are available on the Superintendent’s Screening Committee Web site at (http://www. getinvolvedineducation.com/screening/forms.htm). Additional school information can be found at (http://www.browardschools.com), click on “School Info.”

    If you have any questions, please contact the Parents, Business & Community Partnerships department at 754-321-1970. We look forward to working with you.

    Sincerely,

    Wanda Robinson
    Specialist, Parents, Business & Community Partnerships
